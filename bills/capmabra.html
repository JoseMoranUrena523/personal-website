A BILL

To formally recognize the Civil Air Patrol as a uniformed military auxiliary of the United States Air Force, to provide appropriate federal benefits to active members, and to preserve CAP’s volunteer, nonprofit, and non-combatant character.

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. Short title.

This Act may be cited as the "Civil Air Patrol Military Auxiliary Benefits and Recognition Act of ____".

SEC. 2. Recognition of Civil Air Patrol.

(a) The Civil Air Patrol (CAP) is hereby recognized as a uniformed, volunteer, non-combatant auxiliary of the United States Air Force.

(b) Members of the CAP, both cadets and senior members, shall be designated as uniformed auxiliary personnel of the Department of the Air Force, entitled to receive such federal recognition and auxiliary benefits as authorized herein.

(c) The Civil Air Patrol shall remain a federally chartered nonprofit corporation, governed in accordance with its Constitution and Bylaws, and its volunteer and noncombatant nature shall not be altered by this Act.

SEC. 3. Authorized auxiliary benefits.

(a) The Secretary of the Air Force shall, in coordination with CAP National Headquarters, establish policies for issuing to active CAP members:

(1) A federally recognized, REAL ID–compliant identification card denoting auxiliary status;

(2) Eligibility for a Common Access Card (CAC) or Personal Identity Verification (PIV) card, subject to background screening, mission requirement, and timebound access tied to written orders;

(3) Eligibility for a .gov or .mil email address for official Civil Air Patrol correspondence, granted to cadets and senior members who meet a minimum rank or assignment requirement, as determined and administered by CAP National Headquarters in coordination with the Department of the Air Force;

(4) Healthcare coverage for injuries or illness sustained during any authorized CAP activity, including but not limited to training, meetings, encampments, missions, or exercises;

(5) Access to Department of Defense facilities and systems, for the purpose of CAP operations, with proper credentialing and valid mission assignment.

(b) All elective benefits, including identification credentials, email access, and digital systems participation, shall be optional. All members shall be automatically covered for medical expenses incurred as a direct result of injuries or illnesses sustained during authorized CAP activities.

Eligibility for elective benefits may be limited to active members who meet participation thresholds as defined by CAP National Headquarters. Emergency medical coverage shall apply to all members engaged in authorized CAP activities, regardless of participation status.

SEC. 4. Eligibility and participation requirements.

(a) CAP National Headquarters shall define annual participation standards required for benefit eligibility, which may include minimum mission hours, training events, or verified service contributions.

(b) Members who do not meet participation standards may retain CAP membership but shall not be eligible for federal benefits until reactivation.

(c) The Secretary of the Air Force shall ensure eligibility and verification systems are integrated into CAP’s eServices platform and DoD infrastructure where applicable.

SEC. 5. Preservation of organizational structure.

(a) Nothing in this Act shall affect the nonprofit, volunteer, and independent nature of the Civil Air Patrol.

(b) CAP may continue to receive charitable contributions, sponsorships, and private grants.

(c) CAP shall retain full authority over its cadet programs, aerospace education, and emergency services as defined in federal charter and public law.

SEC. 6. Funding and implementation.

(a) The Secretary of the Air Force shall fund implementation using:

(1) Existing federal CAP appropriations;

(2) Modest increases in CAP national dues, subject to approval by the CAP Board of Governors;

(3) Optional, member-paid fees for CAC/PIV credentials, email hosting, and other benefit-related services.

(b) The Congressional Budget Office is encouraged to score this Act as having minimal fiscal impact due to the volunteer structure and cost-sharing design.

SEC. 7. Annual reporting.

The Civil Air Patrol National Headquarters, in coordination with the Secretary of the Air Force, shall submit to Congress an annual report detailing:

(1) Total number of members receiving federal auxiliary benefits;

(2) Cost of program administration and benefit delivery;

(3) Reports of misuse, credential abuse, or disciplinary concerns;

(4) Summary of program impact on mission readiness and public service.
